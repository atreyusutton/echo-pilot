# How to Contact Local Businesses in Boulder

## Overview
This guide provides strategies for connecting with Boulder businesses to promote EchoPilot. Focus on businesses with high review volume: restaurants, salons, gyms, auto shops, and Airbnb property managers.

---

## 1. Local Business Associations

### Boulder Chamber of Commerce
- **Website:** boulderchamber.com
- **Membership:** $300-500/year
- **Benefits:**
  - Access to member directory
  - Networking events (lunch mixers, after-hours)
  - Can sponsor events for visibility
  - Credibility as a local business
- **ROI:** High - Direct access to decision makers

### Downtown Boulder Business Improvement District (BID)
- **Focus:** Pearl Street businesses
- **Contact:** downtownboulder.com
- **Benefits:** Monthly meetings, represents retail/restaurant owners

### Boulder Small Business Development Center (SBDC)
- **Website:** bouldercounty.gov/business/sbdc
- **Strategy:** Partner with them to offer workshops
- **Benefit:** Free credibility, access to their network

### Industry-Specific Groups
- Boulder Restaurant Association
- Colorado Restaurant Association (Boulder chapter)
- Boulder County Latino Chamber of Commerce
- Women's Chamber of Commerce of Boulder County

### B. Google Maps Mining

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Search "restaurants in Boulder" on Google Maps
2. Filter for 4-5 star businesses (they care about reviews)
3. Look for businesses with 50+ reviews
4. Create spreadsheet:
   - Business name
   - Owner name (if visible)
   - Email (from website)
   - Phone
   - # of reviews
   - Website URL

**Tools to Find Emails:**
- Check their website contact page
- LinkedIn search: "[Business Name] owner Boulder"
- Hunter.io (free tier: 50 searches/month)
- Apollo.io (free tier: limited)

**Goal:** List of 100 target businesses
